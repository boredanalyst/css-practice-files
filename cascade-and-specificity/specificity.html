CSS Cascades and Specificity

A lot of things can happen if you don't pay attention to specificity rules.

Specificity

You can provide a specificity score to a style declaration by counting how many identifiers, classes, or element selectors were used.

Each time an identifier, class, or element was used in the style declaration, the specificity score increases by one.

Often, the style declaration with highest specificity score wins if there are two or more conflicting style declarations.
